# Merch Strategy — Phased Physical & Apparel Plan

> **Status:** Draft (March 2026)
> **Principle:** Every physical object is a world-building artifact, not a logo on a blank.
> **Rule:** No inventory risk until demand is proven. Print-on-demand and pre-order models first.

---

## Why Merch, Why Now

Merch serves three distinct purposes at this stage — and none of them are "make money selling t-shirts":

1. **Sync pitching tool.** A physical object mailed to a sync agency A&R contact is nearly impossible to throw away. USB cards and zines are conversation starters that pitch emails cannot be.
2. **World-building extension.** The site, the Scene series, the music — merch is another entry point into the world. A hand-numbered zine or a matte black USB card signals artistic intention that streaming alone cannot.
3. **Fan conversion.** Listeners who buy something physical become invested. They're more likely to save tracks, share posts, and show up for future releases. Merch converts passive listeners into active supporters.

**What merch is NOT at this stage:** A revenue stream. At current audience size, merch revenue will be negligible. The value is strategic — pitching, world-building, and deepening fan connection. Revenue becomes meaningful at Phase 3+ scale.

## Phase 1: Limited Physical Objects — $200-500

**When:** Afterglow release cycle (post-May 14, when mixing/mastering budget is allocated)
**Quantity:** 50-100 units per item
**Distribution:** Bandcamp + direct mail to sync contacts

### USB Card

| Spec | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Form factor | Credit-card sized USB (16GB minimum) |
| Material | Matte black, logo embossed or UV printed |
| Contents | Afterglow EP in lossless (FLAC/WAV), micro-film (MP4), visual PDF lookbook |
| Quantity | 50-100 units |
| Estimated cost | $3-6/unit at 100 qty (suppliers: USB Memory Direct, CustomUSB, Alibaba for bulk) |
| Total | $300-600 for 100 units |
| Pricing | $15-20 on Bandcamp (perceived value is high — lossless + exclusive visual content) |

**Why USB cards work for you:**
- Impossible to stream. Genuine scarcity.
- The matte black + embossed aesthetic matches your visual world exactly.
- Pre-loaded content means it's not just a storage device — it's a curated experience.
- 5-10 go directly to sync agency A&R contacts with your pitch package. Physical objects get opened.

**Production notes:**
- Design the card layout yourself (Illustrator/Canva) — logo on front, tracklist on back.
- Include a `README.txt` with streaming links, site URL, and a short personal note.
- Pre-load files before shipping. Test on both Mac and PC.
- Custom packaging: black kraft envelope, hand-stamped with logo. Adds $0.50-1/unit.

### Hand-Numbered Zine

| Spec | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Format | A5 or half-letter, saddle-stitched (stapled spine) |
| Pages | 16-24 pages |
| Content | Session photography + lyrics printed as poetry + production notes in scene language |
| Paper | Uncoated stock, 100-120gsm (tactile, matte feel) |
| Quantity | 50-100 units |
| Estimated cost | $2-5/unit for 100 qty (local print shop or Mixam/PrintNinja for short runs) |
| Total | $200-500 for 100 units |
| Pricing | $10-15 on Bandcamp, or bundle with USB card for $25 |
| Special | Each copy hand-numbered (e.g., "23/100"). Adds collectibility at zero cost. |

**Why zines work for you:**
- You already have the photography (session photos, collages from the gallery).
- Lyrics as poetry reframes the music — listeners engage with the words differently on paper.
- Hand-numbered = each one is unique. Scarcity without artificial limitation.
- The 2k17 Exhibitions / Zine concept from the roadmap can be a future edition.

**Production notes:**
- Layout in InDesign, Canva, or even a well-structured Google Doc exported to PDF.
- Include a QR code on the back cover linking to oluanuakin.me.
- Photography should be reproduced at 300 DPI minimum for print clarity.
- Consider including one exclusive photo not on the website — gives buyers something the site doesn't have.

### Cassette (Optional — Evaluate Later)

| Spec | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Supplier | National Audio Company (Springfield, MO) — handles small runs |
| Quantity | 25-50 units (minimum viable run) |
| Estimated cost | $4-8/unit for 50 qty (including J-card printing) |

**Verdict:** Park this. USB card + zine are higher priority and more aligned with your brand. Cassette is a nice-to-have if Phase 1 sells well. The analog aesthetic fits, but cassettes are niche even within indie — don't produce unless there's demand signal.

## Phase 2: Print-on-Demand Apparel — $0 Upfront

**When:** After Afterglow release, once visual identity has 3+ months of consistent social presence
**Platform:** Printful integrated with Bandcamp (or standalone Shopify store — see Platform Decision below)
**Inventory risk:** Zero. Printed and shipped per order.

### How Printful Works

1. You upload designs to Printful
2. Connect to your storefront (Bandcamp or Shopify)
3. Customer orders → Printful prints → Printful ships directly
4. You set retail price. Printful charges base cost. Margin is yours.
5. No inventory, no upfront cost, no shipping logistics.

### Product Line (Start Small)

| Product | Base Cost (Printful) | Retail Price | Margin | Notes |
|---------|---------------------|-------------|--------|-------|
| Heavyweight tee (Comfort Colors 1717) | ~$12-14 | $35-40 | ~$21-26 | Your aesthetic = oversized, washed feel. Comfort Colors fits. |
| Hoodie (Independent Trading Co. IND4000) | ~$22-26 | $55-65 | ~$33-39 | Premium blank. Winter drop timing. |
| Tote bag (canvas) | ~$8-10 | $20-25 | ~$12-15 | Low commitment purchase. Good gateway item. |
| Poster (18x24 or 24x36) | ~$6-8 | $20-25 | ~$14-17 | Gallery collages printed large. Minimal design effort — art already exists. |

**Design approach:**
- **No generic "artist name on a blank tee."** Every piece should feel like it belongs in the world.
- Use collage art from the gallery as full-front or back prints.
- Typography: the same pixel/mono font from the PWR cover art and site.
- Color palette: black, off-white, rust (#A84B2A). No bright colors. No gradients.
- Start with **2-3 designs maximum.** One tee, one poster, maybe one tote. Expand only if they sell.

**Design rules:**
- If you wouldn't wear it yourself, don't sell it.
- If it requires explanation ("oh that's from my EP"), it's not good enough. The design should stand on its own as art.
- No Spotify codes, QR codes, or "stream now" language on wearables. That's advertising, not merch.

### Printful Setup Checklist

- [ ] Create Printful account (free)
- [ ] Upload 2-3 designs (min 300 DPI, PNG with transparency)
- [ ] Order samples of each product (Printful offers 20% sample discount)
- [ ] Photograph samples on-body for the store listing (Scene-series-quality photos)
- [ ] Connect to storefront
- [ ] Set pricing (aim for 60-70% margin to account for occasional promos)

## Phase 3: Vinyl (Qrates Pre-Order) — $0 Upfront

**When:** Only after Afterglow is fully released and there's demonstrated demand (email list growth, social engagement, direct requests)
**Platform:** Qrates (qrates.com) — crowdfunded vinyl pressing
**Inventory risk:** Zero if using pre-order model. Qrates only presses if the minimum is met.

### How Qrates Works

1. You upload audio + artwork + set specs
2. Set a pre-order goal (e.g., 100 copies minimum)
3. Fans pre-order during a campaign window (typically 30 days)
4. If goal is met → Qrates presses and ships to backers
5. If goal is NOT met → all orders refunded, no pressing, no cost to you
6. You can also do "press-then-sell" if you want to fund upfront (skip this — use pre-order model)

### Vinyl Spec

| Spec | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Format | 12" LP (Afterglow EP — even if only 20-25 min, LP format feels premium) |
| Weight | 140g standard (180g costs more, marginal quality difference) |
| Color | Black or custom (translucent smoke, dark amber — match the aesthetic) |
| Jacket | Gatefold if budget allows, otherwise standard single jacket with printed inner sleeve |
| Insert | Lyric sheet / visual booklet (repurpose zine content) |
| Minimum | 100 copies (Qrates minimum for crowdfund) |
| Estimated cost | ~$12-18/unit at 100-300 qty |
| Retail price | $28-35 |
| Margin | ~$10-17/unit |

**Why wait:**
- Vinyl is expensive. A failed Qrates campaign is embarrassing and demoralizing.
- You need a proven audience that will pre-order 100+ copies.
- The Afterglow EP needs to be fully released with all 6 tracks finalized.
- Vinyl turnaround is 8-16 weeks — plan accordingly.

**Demand signals to watch for:**
- 200+ email list subscribers
- Multiple unprompted "is this on vinyl?" DMs/comments
- USB card + zine Phase 1 sells >50% of inventory
- Afterglow has 10k+ total streams across platforms

**Don't launch a Qrates campaign until at least 2 of these 4 signals are present.**

## Platform Decision: Bandcamp vs. Shopify

| Factor | Bandcamp | Shopify |
|--------|----------|---------|
| Monthly cost | $0 | $39/mo (Basic plan) |
| Transaction fee | 10% merch, 15% digital | 2.9% + $0.30 (credit card) |
| Audience | Already there for your music | New destination, must drive traffic |
| Printful integration | Limited (manual or Zapier) | Native Printful app |
| Branding control | Minimal (Bandcamp layout) | Full custom storefront |
| Music + merch bundle | Native feature | Requires workaround |
| Shipping | Built-in label generation | Printful handles (or Shopify Shipping) |

### Recommendation: Bandcamp First, Shopify Later

**Phase 0-2:** Use Bandcamp for everything. Zero monthly cost, your audience is already there, music + merch live together. The 10% cut is higher than Shopify's transaction fees, but you're not paying $39/mo for a store that sells 5 items/month.

**Phase 3+:** Evaluate Shopify only if:
- Monthly merch revenue exceeds $500 (at which point 10% Bandcamp cut > $39 Shopify fee)
- You need a branded storefront experience that Bandcamp can't provide
- Printful integration friction on Bandcamp becomes a bottleneck
- You want to sell non-music items (art prints, exhibition pieces) that don't belong on Bandcamp

**Do NOT start a Shopify store "just to have one."** An empty or sparsely-stocked Shopify store looks worse than selling through Bandcamp. Your site (oluanuakin.me) is the brand hub — it links to Bandcamp for purchases. That's the correct flow until scale justifies a dedicated store.

## What NOT to Do

1. **Don't order 500 t-shirts.** Print-on-demand exists. Use it.
2. **Don't launch merch before you have consistent social presence.** Nobody buys merch from an artist they discovered 5 minutes ago. The Scene series and release cycle build the relationship first.
3. **Don't put your face on merch.** Your collage art, your typography, your visual world — not a photo of yourself on a shirt.
4. **Don't sell merch at a loss "for exposure."** Price for real margin. Your audience respects quality and will pay for it.
5. **Don't open a Shopify store in Phase 0.** That's $468/year for a store with no products and no traffic.
6. **Don't produce cassettes, vinyl, or any physical media without demand signals.** Nostalgia formats are cool but expensive. Let the audience tell you what they want.
