# **The Plan at a Glance**

Two coordinated moments, three weeks apart, with six weeks of social momentum building before the first one. The site launch is the brand moment. The PWR wide release is the discovery moment. They feed each other.

| Milestone | Date | What It Is |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Site Launch** | Friday April 24 | Website goes live with PWR as featured project. Brand moment. Share with inner circle first, public announcement same day. |
| **Spotify Editorial Submit** | Monday May 4 | Submit PWR via Spotify for Artists. 11 days before release. Genre: conscious hip-hop / hip-hop soul. Mood: empowerment, transformation, arrival. |
| **PWR Wide Release** | Friday May 15 | PWR drops on Spotify, Apple Music, Tidal. New cover art (Option B). Bandcamp retains original. Discovery moment. |

*Why Friday? New Music Friday is the biggest editorial playlist on Spotify. All editorial submission windows are built around Friday releases. Release Radar populates on Fridays. Dropping any other day is leaving the biggest discovery mechanism on the table.*

# **Key Decisions (Locked In)**

**PWR distribution:** Go wide. Spotify, Apple Music, Tidal, Amazon Music — plus keep the Bandcamp original. Use your existing distributor (DistroKid, TuneCore, or whatever you’re on). If you’re not on one yet, DistroKid at $22.99/year is the simplest path for unlimited releases.

**Cover art:** Option B — same photo as the current Bandcamp cover, but scale up the “PWR” text 3-4x larger for thumbnail visibility. Keep the pixel/mono font. Consider shifting the green to a brighter, more saturated green or to your rust color. Test it at 60x60 pixels on your phone before finalizing — if you can’t read “PWR” at that size, the type isn’t big enough.

**Artist profile photo:** Keep the blue motion-blur portrait through the entire PWR cycle. Do not change it until Afterglow drops. The profile change becomes part of the Afterglow release event.

**Genre tagging for PWR:** Do NOT tag as trap-soul. Tag as: conscious hip-hop, hip-hop soul, alternative soul. In the Spotify editorial pitch, describe it as: “Conscious hip-hop verses over soul production. Personal transformation energy — a character stepping into power.”

**Site:** PWR becomes the featured project in the hero section. Afterglow tracklist section stays but shifts to a “Coming Soon” or “Next Project” framing. Everything else on the site (Gallery, Notes, About, Archive) stays as-is.

# **Week-by-Week Plan**

13 weeks from today (March 14\) through the end of PWR’s release month. Every week has specific actions.

## **Weeks 1-2: March 14 – March 27 — Foundation**

Get the free infrastructure done. No money spent. No excuses.

* Register with SoundExchange (free, 15 min, soundexchange.com)

* Call ASCAP (1-800-952-7227) or register with BMI to resolve signup issue

* Claim Spotify for Artists \+ Apple Music for Artists if not done

* Set up Songlink/Odesli for PWR and all existing tracks (free)

* Update all social bios with genre language: Alternative Soul / Indie R\&B

* Start filming Scene series content. Shoot 5-8 clips this two-week window. You don’t post them all yet — you’re building a backlog.

* Build The Afterglow Palette playlist on Spotify (see detailed guide in Playbook v3.1, Part 3). Pin to profile.

* Begin modifying PWR cover art — scale up type, test at thumbnail size

## **Weeks 3-4: March 28 – April 10 — Content Engine Starts**

The Scene series goes live. You start showing up consistently.

* Start posting Scene series: 3-4 posts per week across Instagram and TikTok

* Format: 30-60 second cinematic clip, your music underneath, title card (“Scene 01 — The Arrival”), no caption explaining anything

* Use PWR as the audio on at least 2 of the first batch — seed it into the algorithm as original audio

* Finish the PWR cover art variant. Get it to 3000x3000 pixels minimum for distribution.

* Finalize which distributor you’re using. If new, sign up for DistroKid ($22.99/year).

* Register PWR with the Copyright Office ($35-65). Do this before the wide release.

* Reach out to 5-10 independent filmmakers on Vimeo/Instagram — offer PWR for a project

## **Weeks 5-6: April 11 – April 24 — Site Prep \+ Launch**

Final site tweaks and the launch itself.

* Make the site changes listed above: hero swap, Afterglow section reframe, archive links prep

* Test the site on mobile thoroughly. Your older demographic target means you cannot skip this.

* Create the EPK as a one-page PDF. You have every asset from the site already.

* Upload PWR to your distributor. Set release date as May 15\. Most distributors need 2-4 weeks lead time — upload by April 20 at the latest.

* FRIDAY APRIL 24: Site goes live. Share with your inner circle first (DM 10-20 people who care). Then post publicly. One clean announcement — not “check out my new website,” but something that fits your world. A screenshot, a 10-second screen recording scrolling through, your music playing underneath.

* Continue Scene series at 3-4 posts/week. You should have 12-16 posts live by now.

## **Weeks 7-8: April 25 – May 8 — Bridge Period**

The site is live. PWR is uploaded and waiting. This is the build-up window.

* MONDAY MAY 4: Submit PWR to Spotify editorial via Spotify for Artists. Use the pitch: “Conscious hip-hop verses over soul production. PWR is about personal transformation — stepping into power after being done performing exhaustion. Scene: a character who’s been quiet finally arrives. Summer energy, conscious and grounded.”

* Launch a Groover campaign ($20-40): target 10-20 curators in hip-hop soul, conscious rap, alternative soul, cinematic playlists

* Tease PWR’s streaming arrival on socials. Not a countdown — something subtler. A 15-second clip from PWR over a Scene-series-style visual with a date: “05.15”

* Continue Scene series. Start using more PWR clips in posts to build familiarity with the track before it’s available to save.

* Email your list (even if it’s small) about the upcoming release. Frame it as early access to something: “Pre-save link below. You hear it first.”

* Set up a pre-save link through your distributor or a tool like DistroKid’s HyperFollow. Share on stories.

## **Week 9: May 9 – May 15 — RELEASE WEEK**

*The first 72 hours after release are the most important window for Spotify’s algorithm. Every save, every playlist add, every repeat listen in this window counts disproportionately. Coordinate everything to land in these 72 hours.*

* MONDAY-THURSDAY: Final tease posts. One per day. Building to Friday.

* FRIDAY MAY 15 — RELEASE DAY:

* Post the announcement across all platforms. Link to the Songlink/Odesli URL (routes each listener to their platform automatically).

* Instagram: Reel with a Scene-series-quality visual \+ PWR \+ the link sticker. Story with Songlink sticker. Feed post with the new cover art.

* TikTok: PWR as original audio on a new post. Pin it to your profile.

* Update your website: Add Spotify/Apple/Tidal links to the PWR streaming row in the Archive section. Update the hero CTA link.

* DM your core supporters directly. Ask them to save the track (not just stream it). Saves are the strongest algorithm signal in 2026\.

* Text your real friends. The people who actually care. Ask them to save it on Spotify. 20-30 genuine saves in the first 48 hours matter more than 200 passive streams.

* SATURDAY-SUNDAY: Follow up on stories. Share any early reactions, playlist adds, or screenshots of people listening. Social proof compounds.

## **Weeks 10-13: May 16 – June 12 — Ride the Momentum**

The release is out. Now you sustain it instead of letting it fade after one weekend.

* Continue Scene series — but now every post uses PWR as the audio. You’re training the algorithm to associate your visual content with this track.

* Check Spotify for Artists analytics weekly. Look at: save rate, repeat listen ratio, skip rate in first 30 seconds, source of streams (algorithmic vs external). If algorithmic streams start appearing, the strategy is working.

* Submit to SubmitHub ($20-30) targeting blogs and playlist curators in your lane. Use scene language in the pitch.

* Follow up with filmmakers from week 3-4 outreach. Anyone who responded positively gets the streaming link and an offer to use PWR in their project.

* If the Groover campaign generated any blog coverage or playlist adds, share those on socials as social proof.

* Start planning the Afterglow rollout based on everything you’ve learned — what content performed, what didn’t, which curators responded, which platform drove the most engagement.

# **PWR Cover Art Spec (Option B)**

For whoever is producing the final file — whether that’s you in Photoshop/Canva or a designer:

* Source image: the existing PWR Bandcamp cover photo (warm amber skin tones, dark background)

* Resolution: minimum 3000x3000 pixels (Spotify requires 3000x3000, Apple requires at least 1400x1400)

* Format: JPG or PNG, sRGB color space

* Type treatment: scale “PWR” text to roughly 3-4x current size. It should be easily readable at 60x60 pixel thumbnail. Keep the pixel/mono font style.

* Color options for type: (1) keep the green but more saturated/brighter, or (2) shift to your rust (\#A84B2A) for tighter brand connection. Test both at thumbnail size on your phone before choosing.

* Do NOT add artist name to the cover. Spotify and Apple display the artist name separately — putting it on the cover is redundant and clutters the image.

* Do NOT add parental advisory labels, streaming platform logos, or URLs.

* The Bandcamp version keeps its original artwork. Two editions: the intimate original and the streaming-optimized variant.

# **What Success Looks Like**

After 13 weeks, here’s what you’re measuring. These aren’t vanity metrics — they’re the signals that tell you whether to double down or adjust before Afterglow.

## **Spotify (check in Spotify for Artists)**

* Save rate above 3% (saves divided by total listeners). Above 5% is exceptional.

* Repeat listen ratio above 1.5 (streams per listener). Above 2.0 means people are coming back.

* Any algorithmic source streams appearing (Discover Weekly, Radio, Daily Mix). Even a small amount means the algorithm noticed you.

* At least 2-3 user-generated playlist adds beyond your own.

## **Social**

* Scene series has 20+ posts live across Instagram/TikTok

* At least 1-2 posts that notably outperformed the others (pay attention to what those had in common — that’s your content formula)

* Inbound DMs or comments from filmmakers, visual artists, or curators who found you through the series

## **Website**

* Steady traffic from social links (check your hosting analytics or add a free Plausible/Umami tracker)

* Email signups growing (even 5-10 real signups in the first month is meaningful at this stage)

* At least one inbound inquiry from the Contact or Sync section

## **Filmmaker Outreach**

* At least 2-3 responses from your 5-10 outreach messages

* At least 1 actual use of your track in someone’s project (even a student film or spec piece)

If these signals are present after 13 weeks, you have a working engine. Afterglow launches into an existing audience, a proven social content formula, an active Spotify profile, and real momentum — instead of launching into nothing.
